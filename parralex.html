EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only celestial body known to harbor life. It has a diameter of about 12,742 kilometers, orbits the Sun at an average distance of 150 million kilometers (1 AU), and completes one orbit every 365.25 days. Earth’s unique combination of a protective atmosphere, abundant liquid water, and a magnetic field makes it a rare oasis in the solar system.

About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by oceans, while the remaining land hosts vast continents, mountains, deserts, and forests. Its atmosphere is composed mainly of 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen, which supports respiration and shields the planet from harmful solar radiation. Earth’s axial tilt of 23.5° gives rise to changing seasons, while its single natural satellite, the Moon, influences tides and helps stabilize Earth’s rotation.
